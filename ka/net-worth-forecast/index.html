How the Forecast Engine Works

NetWorthCast doesn't use a single growth rate for your entire portfolio. Each piece of your financial picture is modeled individually:

  • Every asset compounds on its own — your 401(k) might grow at 8%, your savings account at 4%, and your real estate at 3%. Each asset uses its own expected return rate.
  • Revenue and expenses have annual growth rates — salary increases, rent hikes, and subscription creep are all factored into the projection year over year.
  • One-time events are built in — model a signing bonus in year 2, a home purchase in year 5, or a car replacement in year 8. These events land exactly when you schedule them.
  • Cascade withdrawal logic — when expenses exceed income in retirement, the forecast draws down from liquid assets first, preserving illiquid holdings as long as possible.

The result is a projection that reflects how money actually works — not a flat-line estimate based on one number.

Forecast Scenarios You Can Model

The real power of a net worth forecast is comparing "what if" scenarios. Adjust any input and see the 30-year impact instantly:

What if I get a raise?

Increase your income and see how higher savings compound over decades. A $10k raise invested at 8% adds over $120k in 10 years.

What if I buy a house?

Model the down payment as a one-time event, add mortgage payments to expenses, and add the property as an illiquid asset with its own growth rate.

What if I reduce expenses?

Cutting $500/month frees $6,000/year for investments. Over 20 years at 8% growth, that's an extra $274k in net worth.

What if I relocate?

Moving to a lower cost-of-living area can dramatically accelerate your timeline. Compare cities and see the forecast shift in real time.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does a net worth forecast work?

A net worth forecast projects your financial future by compounding each asset individually at its own expected return rate, applying annual growth rates to income and expenses, and accounting for one-time events like bonuses or large purchases. NetWorthCast runs this projection up to 30 years into the future.

What is the difference between tracking net worth and forecasting it?

Tracking looks backward — it records where your money has been. Forecasting looks forward — it projects where your money is going based on compound growth, savings rates, and planned life events. Both are essential, but forecasting lets you make better decisions today.

How accurate is a 30-year net worth projection?

No projection is perfectly accurate over 30 years, but it gives you a realistic range based on your current trajectory. The real value is in comparing scenarios — seeing how a raise, a home purchase, or reduced expenses changes your long-term outcome. Update your inputs regularly for the most useful projections.

Can I model different financial scenarios?

Yes. NetWorthCast lets you adjust income, expenses, asset return rates, and one-time events to model scenarios like getting a raise, buying a house, relocating to a cheaper city, or cutting expenses. Each change updates the 30-year forecast instantly so you can compare outcomes side by side.
