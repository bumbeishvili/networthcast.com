인기 비교

New York City vs Lisbon

A single person in NYC spends roughly $4,800/month on essentials. The same lifestyle in Lisbon costs around $2,200/month — a 54% reduction. Rent alone drops from ~$2,800 to ~$1,100. Portugal's Non-Habitual Resident tax regime can further reduce your effective tax rate.

San Francisco vs Bangkok

San Francisco averages $5,500/month for a single person. Bangkok comes in around $1,400/month — roughly 75% cheaper. Food costs drop dramatically, and healthcare is high quality at a fraction of the US price. Thailand's digital nomad visa makes long-term stays viable.

London vs Tbilisi

London runs about $4,200/month. Tbilisi averages $1,100/month — a 74% savings. Georgia offers a territorial tax system (foreign-source income is not taxed) and one of the most welcoming visa regimes for remote workers.

Dubai vs Bali

Dubai's high rents push monthly costs to about $3,600 for a single person, despite 0% income tax. Bali averages $1,300/month with a similar lifestyle. The trade-off: Dubai offers world-class infrastructure, while Bali offers nature and a slower pace — both with favorable tax treatment for many nationalities.

For Remote Workers & Digital Nomads

If you earn in USD, EUR, or GBP and can work from anywhere, cost of living comparison becomes your most powerful financial tool. This strategy — earning in a strong currency while spending in a weaker one — is called geographic arbitrage.

Visa Considerations

Many cities on our list now offer digital nomad visas or freelancer permits: Portugal, Spain, Georgia, Estonia, Thailand, Colombia, and the UAE all have dedicated programs. Visa type affects your tax obligations, so always check local requirements.

Tax Implications

Moving abroad doesn't automatically eliminate your home-country tax obligations. US citizens are taxed on worldwide income. EU residents may trigger tax residency after 183 days. Countries like Georgia, Dubai, and Paraguay offer territorial or zero income tax — but the rules are nuanced. NetWorthCast includes estimated tax rates per city to help you plan.

Geographic Arbitrage & FIRE

Combining remote work with a low cost-of-living base can cut years off your FIRE timeline. If your expenses drop by 50%, your FIRE number drops by 50% — and your savings rate jumps, compounding the effect. Use NetWorthCast to model the impact of relocation on your 30-year financial forecast.

자주 묻는 질문

What is a cost of living comparison?

A cost of living comparison measures how expensive it is to maintain a similar standard of living in different cities or countries. It covers rent, groceries, transport, utilities, healthcare, and taxes. NetWorthCast compares 44 cities across 11 regions so you can see where your money goes furthest.

How does the cost of living calculator work?

Enter your income and family size. The calculator scales costs for each city based on household size and shows a monthly breakdown of rent, food, transport, utilities, healthcare, other expenses, and estimated tax. Compare two cities side by side or browse all 44 ranked by total monthly cost.

Which city has the lowest cost of living?

Among our 44 cities, Chiang Mai, Sofia, Goa, and Da Nang consistently rank as the most affordable. But the cheapest city for you depends on your lifestyle, family size, and which expense categories matter most to you.

Does cost of living include taxes?

예. NetWorthCast includes an estimated effective tax rate for each city so you compare take-home purchasing power, not just pre-tax costs. Tax rates range from 0% in Dubai to over 40% in parts of Western Europe.

How does cost of living affect my FIRE number?

Your FIRE number is 25x your annual expenses. Relocating from a $5,000/month city to a $2,500/month city cuts your FIRE number from $1.5M to $750K — roughly halving your time to financial independence.

What is geographic arbitrage?

Geographic arbitrage means earning in a strong currency while living in a lower-cost location. Remote workers use this to save more, invest more, and reach financial independence faster. Learn more about geographic arbitrage.
