How to Calculate Net Worth

The formula is straightforward — add up all your assets, subtract all your liabilities, and the result is your net worth:

Net Worth = Total Assets − Total Liabilities

Liquid assets are things you can convert to cash quickly: savings accounts, stocks, bonds, crypto, and retirement accounts. These drive your financial independence timeline.

Illiquid assets are valuable but harder to sell: real estate, vehicles, business equity, and collectibles. They contribute to total net worth but can't easily cover living expenses.

NetWorthCast tracks both separately and lets you assign individual growth rates to each asset — so your stock portfolio can compound at 8% while your home appreciates at 3%.

What Counts as Assets

Include everything of value that you own:

  • Cash & savings — checking accounts, savings accounts, emergency fund
  • Investments — stocks, bonds, index funds, ETFs, mutual funds
  • Retirement accounts — 401(k), IRA, Roth IRA, pension
  • Cryptocurrency — Bitcoin, Ethereum, and other digital assets
  • Real estate — primary home, rental properties, land
  • Vehicles — cars, motorcycles, boats
  • Other — business equity, collectibles, jewelry, intellectual property

Then subtract your liabilities: mortgage balance, student loans, auto loans, credit card debt, personal loans, medical debt, and any other money you owe.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is net worth?

Net worth is the total value of everything you own (assets) minus everything you owe (liabilities). It is the single best measure of your overall financial health. A positive net worth means your assets exceed your debts, while a negative net worth means you owe more than you own.

How do you calculate net worth?

Net Worth = Total Assets - Total Liabilities. Add up all your assets (cash, savings, investments, real estate, vehicles) and subtract all your liabilities (mortgage, student loans, credit card debt, car loans). The result is your net worth.

What counts as assets and liabilities?

Assets include cash, savings accounts, stocks, bonds, 401(k), IRA, cryptocurrency, real estate, vehicles, and personal property. Liabilities include mortgages, student loans, auto loans, credit card balances, personal loans, and any other debts you owe.

What is the average net worth by age?

Average net worth benchmarks vary widely, but general targets are: ~$20,000 by age 25, ~$100,000 by 30, ~$250,000 by 35, ~$500,000 by 40, ~$1,000,000 by 50, and ~$2,000,000 by 60. These figures depend heavily on income, savings rate, and investment returns.
