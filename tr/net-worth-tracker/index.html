What You Can Track

NetWorthCast handles both liquid and illiquid assets, each with its own expected return rate. This gives you an accurate picture of your total net worth and how each piece grows over time.

Liquid Assets
Cash, HYSA, 401(k), IRA, Brokerage, Stocks, Crypto

Assets you can convert to cash relatively quickly. These are what count toward financial independence.

Illiquid Assets
Real Estate, Vehicles, Collectibles

Assets tied up in physical property. They contribute to total net worth but aren't readily available for spending.

Each asset gets its own expected annual return rate. A high-yield savings account might earn 4.5%, a stock portfolio 8%, crypto 12%, and a vehicle -15%. This makes your projections far more accurate than using a single blended rate.

How NetWorthCast Tracking Works

1. Add Your Assets

Create each asset with a name, current value, and expected return rate. For example: "Fidelity 401k — $85,000 — 7% return" or "Primary Home — $420,000 — 3.5% appreciation."

2. Set Individual Return Rates

Every asset compounds at its own rate. Your cash earns differently than your stocks, and your car depreciates. NetWorthCast models each one independently for realistic 30-year projections.

3. Designate a Primary Account

Pick one liquid account (typically checking or HYSA) as your primary. This is where income flows in and expenses flow out. When expenses exceed income in a given month, the shortfall cascades from this account first.

4. Reorder Accounts

Drag accounts into your preferred order. The order determines the cascade priority — if your primary account runs dry, expenses pull from the next liquid account in line.
